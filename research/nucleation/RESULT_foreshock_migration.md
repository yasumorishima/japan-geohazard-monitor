# A real (weak) spatial precursor: foreshocks migrate specifically toward the eventual mainshock — SoCal population

## Why this matters
The b-value (magnitude) and rate channels of the foreshock-traffic-light arc are NULL across five cases and a ~150-mainshock population test: no FTLS RED, no acceleration beyond ordinary triggering. This is the SPATIAL channel, and the first POSITIVE result in the arc. The one foreshock precursor with credible positive literature is MIGRATION toward the nucleation point (Kato et al. 2012 Tohoku, 2016 Iquique, slow-slip-driven on megathrusts). Here we test it as a population on a dense crustal catalogue and -- after passing every adversarial control a reviewer demanded -- find a weak but real, mainshock-specific spatial tendency.

## Method
SoCal QTM catalogue (Ross et al. 2019), 898,597 template-matched relocated events 2008-2017 (relative location ~0.1-1 km), magnitudes to 0.01. Mainshocks = M>=4.0 events that are the largest within +-15 d AND 20 km (declustered local maxima). Foreshocks per mainshock = events within 20 km, [MS-15d, MS), M>=1.0, M < Mmainshock-0.3, time-ordered. Migration metric = per-sequence Spearman rho(foreshock time, epicentral distance-to-mainshock); rho<0 = foreshocks get CLOSER over time (migration toward). Population inference = Wilcoxon/t-test on the per-sequence rho distribution (the sequence is the unit, n=52 at baseline). This is the pre-specified PRIMARY spec; an 8-cell parameter sweep is reported only as (non-independent) sensitivity.

## Result and the controls that it survives
**Baseline:** mean rho = -0.098, median -0.080, 67% of sequences rho<0, Wilcoxon p = 0.0061; 21% (11/52) individually significant at a per-sequence time-shuffle permutation p<0.05 (vs 5% by chance, ~4x). Median last-third-minus-first-third distance = -0.19 km. The effect is robust in SIGN across all 8 parameter settings (mean rho -0.077 to -0.114; significant in 5/8, marginal p 0.09-0.12 in the smallest-n / smallest-R / shortest-T cells).

Every blocking control demanded by adversarial review passes:
| control | result | meaning |
|---|---|---|
| (1) SYNTHETIC uniform null (same counts, uniform-in-disk + uniform-time, real mainshock loc, full pipeline) | mean rho = +0.005 (p=0.85) | the pipeline/metric does NOT manufacture rho<0 -- the decisive artifact test is clean |
| (2) reference = mainshock | -0.098 (p=0.006) | the contraction is real |
| (2) reference = foreshock centroid | +0.006 (p=0.73) | NOT a general contraction toward the cluster centre |
| (2) reference = random foreshock | -0.038 (p=0.34) | not toward an arbitrary point |
| (2) reference = first foreshock | +0.165 (p<1e-4) | foreshocks EXPAND from the first event (Omori-like) |
| (3) partial Spearman controlling magnitude | -0.106 (p=0.006) | NOT a later=larger=better-located artifact |
| (3) narrow magnitude band (Mc..Mc+0.5) | -0.159 (p=0.032) | survives, stronger, with magnitude held nearly fixed |
| (4) 3D hypocentral distance (depth included) | -0.078 (p=0.076) | survives in 3D (depth adds noise; marginal) |

The decisive points: the synthetic uniform null is clean (+0.005), so the negative rho is not a pipeline artifact; and the contraction is SPECIFIC to the eventual mainshock location (-0.098) -- it vanishes against the foreshock centroid (+0.006) or a random point (-0.038). So foreshocks approach the FUTURE HYPOCENTRE specifically, not the cluster in general, and not because later events are larger/better-located. As an independent validity check, the IDENTICAL metric applied to AFTERSHOCKS gives the opposite, expected sign (mean rho +0.121, 72% expanding = classic Omori-Utsu aftershock-zone expansion; foreshock-vs-aftershock Mann-Whitney p<1e-4).

## Honest scope
- **Weak.** rho ~ -0.1 explains ~1% of rank variance; the median approach (-0.19 km) is comparable to the catalogue location precision. The signal is real and direction-coherent but per-sequence tiny -- NOT operationally useful for hypocentre forecasting.
- **Mechanism-agnostic.** This is catalogue geometry only; we have no geodetic/aseismic-slip evidence here. The result is CONSISTENT with slow-slip-driven nucleation loading (the Kato interpretation) but also with cascade/static-triggering concentrating activity onto the nucleation patch; the data cannot distinguish them. We do not claim slow slip, and we note the Kato megathrust mechanism need not be the crustal one.
- **Selection framing.** "Migration" here means later foreshocks lie nearer the eventual epicentre; whether that is continuous propagation or late concentration at the nucleation patch is not resolved (both are precursory in the same operational sense).
- **Not a magnitude/rate result.** This does NOT revive the FTLS b-RED (still null); it is a distinct, spatial channel.

## Conclusion
On a ~50-sequence SoCal crustal population, foreshocks migrate weakly but significantly and SPECIFICALLY toward the eventual mainshock hypocentre (mean Spearman rho ~ -0.1, mainshock-specific, magnitude-controlled, 3D-robust, opposite to aftershock expansion, and absent from a synthetic no-migration null). This is the arc's first positive precursor channel: where the magnitude (b-value) and rate channels carry no open-data signal, the SPATIAL channel does -- a genuine, if weak and operationally-modest, directed tendency consistent with (but not proof of) nucleation-zone loading, generalising the qualitative Kato megathrust migration to a crustal population at population scale.

Catalogue qtm12.hypo (SCEDC QTM 12dev, Ross et al. 2019). ## Cross-region generalisation (2026-06-24)
The SoCal result replicates in an independent region/catalogue and is shown not to be SoCal-specific:
- **Northern California (Waldhauser-Schaff NCAeqDD, 513,474 relocated events 1984-2011, Mc 1.5; different fault systems -- creeping San Andreas, Geysers geothermal, Mendocino, Long Valley):** foreshock mainshock-ref rho = -0.113 (Wilcoxon p < 1e-4, n=146 sequences), migration TOWARD and STRONGER than SoCal. Mainshock-SPECIFIC (centroid-ref -0.034 p=0.19, random-ref -0.032 p=0.24, both NS). Synthetic uniform null clean (+0.007, p=0.78). Survives 3D (-0.103, p<1e-4) and magnitude control (partial -0.111, p<1e-4). The aftershock-expansion GATE passes (+0.133, p<1e-4 = classic Omori-Utsu), confirming the catalogue has the resolution.
- **Northern Chile subduction (Sippl IPOC, Mc 2.6, offshore):** NULL but UNINFORMATIVE -- foreshock rho +0.010 (p=0.55) AND the aftershock-expansion gate FAILS (+0.015, p=0.40), so the catalogue cannot detect even the well-established aftershock expansion; its migration null is resolution-limited, not a tectonic difference.
- **Resolution requirement, demonstrated:** degrading the dense SoCal catalogue to higher Mc reproduces the Chile pattern -- both the foreshock migration and the aftershock expansion lose significance by Mc 2.0-2.5 (Mc 2.5: foreshock p=0.18, aftershock p=0.16, n~20) purely from the collapse in well-sampled sequences. The signal is weak and needs a dense, low-Mc, well-located catalogue; offshore high-Mc catalogues are below the detection floor.

Net: foreshock migration toward the eventual mainshock is replicated in TWO independent dense crustal California catalogues (SoCal QTM rho -0.098, p=0.006; NorCal DD rho -0.113, p<1e-4), both mainshock-specific and surviving every control, while the one offshore subduction test is resolution-limited (fails the aftershock gate, as a Mc-degraded SoCal also does). The positive spatial precursor is robust and NOT region-specific within the dense-catalogue regime; cross-tectonic-regime (subduction) generalisation remains untested for lack of a sufficiently complete offshore foreshock catalogue. Assets: RPi5 ~/geo-ml/norcal/migration_norcal.py (NorCal), ~/geo-ml/iquique/migration_chile.py (Chile), ~/geo-ml/qtm/migration_mcdegrade.py (SoCal Mc-degradation). NorCal catalogue NCAeqDD.v201112.1 (Waldhauser & Schaff 2008). Methodology identical to the Opus-reviewed SoCal test (controls pre-approved); NorCal/Chile are replications.

## Characterisation of the migration (2026-06-24, exploratory/descriptive; two catalogues = mutual replication)
- **Velocity (net centroid-approach rate, Theil-Sen km/day):** population median -0.028 (SoCal) / -0.051 (NorCal); migrating-subset (rho<0) median -0.110 (SoCal, n=40) / -0.118 (NorCal, n=101). Even the migrating subset approaches at ~0.1 km/day -- one to two orders of magnitude below documented SSE front-propagation speeds (~1-20 km/day). This is a NET centroid-approach rate, not a front velocity, so the comparison is order-of-magnitude only; it indicates the signal is NOT a fast propagating front. Whether the driver is a slow/episodic aseismic transient or cumulative cascade/stress concentration toward the nucleation patch cannot be distinguished by these data (mechanism-agnostic; the Kato slow-slip analogy is tempered, not excluded).
- **Timing:** the approach concentrates significantly in the SECOND half of the foreshock window -- mean distance-to-mainshock 1st->2nd half drops by a median -0.51 km (SoCal, paired Wilcoxon p=0.020) / -0.52 km (NorCal, p<1e-4). A progressive approach toward the event, consistent with (but not proof of) nucleation-zone loading.
- **No magnitude dependence:** Spearman(mainshock M, per-sequence rho) = -0.019 (SoCal, p=0.89) / +0.068 (NorCal, p=0.42).
- **No demonstrated location-forecasting skill.** Predicting the mainshock epicentre from foreshocks only: the all-foreshock centroid already achieves median error 4.08 km (SoCal) / 3.26 km (NorCal); neither a recency-restricted centroid (last-third / last-5: not better, last-5 SoCal significantly WORSE p=0.04) nor a trajectory extrapolation (linear centroid-path fit extrapolated to the event: SoCal 4.63 km worse p=0.17, NorCal 3.21 km marginal p=0.09) beats it, and each beats the all-centroid in only ~0.49 of sequences (coin-flip). The ~1 km net migration is swamped by the centroid noise of using fewer late events, so the established signal carries no operational localization skill via these predictors.

Net characterisation: the migration is real, weak (rho ~ -0.1), SLOW (~0.1 km/day net, well below SSE front speeds), magnitude-independent, progressively concentrated toward the event, and operationally negligible for hypocentre forecasting -- a scientifically real but practically minor spatial precursor, mechanism-agnostic. Assets: ~/geo-ml/qtm/migration_characterize.py, migration_forecast.py, migration_char2.py. Exploratory/descriptive characterisation; the two catalogues serve as mutual replication.

## Terminal acceleration -- examined, NOT robustly established (honest negative)
A finer look at whether the approach ACCELERATES terminally (the most nucleation-like signature): in raw form both regions show steeper 2nd-half approach slopes (SoCal -0.040 -> -0.146 km/day, paired p=0.049; NorCal +0.016 -> -0.104, p<1e-3) and final-1-day foreshocks closer than earlier ones (SoCal -1.07 km p<1e-3; NorCal -0.74 km p<1e-3). BUT under a narrow-magnitude-band control (final-day and earlier events held at equal magnitude, removing the later=larger=better-located effect), the final-day-closer result survives only in SoCal (-1.62 km at fixed mag, p=0.005) and VANISHES in NorCal (-0.37 km, p=0.51). So terminal acceleration is suggestive but magnitude-confounded -- not a robust two-region finding. The robust claim remains the OVERALL migration (which passed the magnitude-partial control in both regions); the terminal/final-day refinement is region-dependent and partly a location-quality artifact, and is not claimed.

## Cross-region generalisation #3 — Japan inland crustal (JUICE), 2026-06-25
A THIRD independent dense relocated catalogue, in a tectonic setting distinct from California's San-Andreas transform system: **JUICE** (Japan Unified hIgh-resolution relocated Catalog for Earthquakes; Yano, Takeda, Matsubara & Shiomi 2017, *Tectonophysics* 702:19-28, doi:10.1016/j.tecto.2017.02.017). 1,091,640 hypocentres beneath the Japanese islands, 2001-2012, double-difference relocated from Hi-net waveform cross-correlation (horizontal uncertainty ~0.37 km, vertical ~0.85 km). Openly downloadable (hinet.bosai.go.jp/topics/JUICE/). Identical migration spec and full control suite as SoCal/NorCal.

**Catalogue scope / analysis restriction (important).** JUICE is crustal-dominated but its M>=4 set still contains deep subduction-interface and slab events (24.7% of M>=4 are deeper than 30 km, to 83 km). To test *inland crustal* nucleation apples-to-apples with California's inherently shallow catalogues, the **whole catalogue is filtered to depth <= DMAX BEFORE any selection** (mainshocks, foreshocks and aftershocks all crustal). Results are reported at DMAX = 20 km and 15 km; the unfiltered run is given only to show why the restriction matters.

**Main result (depth-restricted, MODE=all, the headline).** The migration replicates and is mainshock-specific:
| cut | gate (aftershock >0) | mainshock-ref | centroid-ref | PAIRED main−centroid | random-ref | synthetic null | 3D | partial \| mag |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| depth<=20 km, MC0.5, n=235 | +0.109 (p<1e-4) | **-0.077 (p3e-4)** | -0.029 (p0.28, NS) | **-0.048 (p6e-4)** | -0.032 (p0.15, NS) | +0.010 (p0.10) | -0.078 (p2e-4) | -0.074 (p4e-4) |
| depth<=15 km, MC0.5, n=206 | +0.112 (p<1e-4) | **-0.086 (p<1e-4)** | -0.037 (p0.10, NS) | **-0.049 (p5e-4)** | -0.032 (p0.21, NS) | +0.007 (p0.91) | -0.087 (p<1e-4) | -0.084 (p<1e-4) |

Every blocking control passes: the synthetic uniform null is clean (the pipeline does not manufacture rho<0), the contraction is SPECIFIC to the eventual mainshock (centroid-ref and random-ref both NS; PAIRED main-minus-centroid significant), it survives magnitude control (partial) and 3D, and the aftershock-expansion GATE passes strongly (+0.11 to +0.14, p<1e-4 — classic Omori-Utsu, so the catalogue has the resolution; cf. Chile which FAILS this gate).

**Depth restriction STRENGTHENS the signal — monotonically.** Unfiltered, the all-mode signal is weaker/marginal (MC0.5 -0.058 p5e-4; MC1.0 -0.033 p0.07; MC1.5 -0.037 p0.024 — see MC note below). Restricting to crustal raises mainshock-ref from -0.058 (unfiltered) to -0.077 (<=20 km) to -0.086 (<=15 km), and converts the borderline MC1.0 to significant. Physically consistent: deep subduction/slab contamination was diluting a genuine inland-crustal signal. At <=15 km the effect (-0.086, partial -0.084) reaches the LOWER END of / approximately matches the California range (SoCal -0.098, NorCal -0.113); we do not claim full equivalence, since that rests on the single tightest cut — but the direction-coherent strengthening as DMAX tightens is itself robustness evidence, not cherry-picking.

**Magnitude-of-completeness sweep (depth<=20 km, all).** Both low cuts are significant and mainshock-specific: MC0.5 mainshock -0.077 (p3e-4), PAIRED -0.048 (p6e-4), partial -0.074 (p4e-4); MC1.0 mainshock -0.047 (p0.018), PAIRED -0.019 (p0.042), partial -0.045 (p0.026), 3D -0.048 (p0.010), synthetic +0.017 clean, random/centroid NS. (The MC1.0 non-monotonicity seen unfiltered, p0.07, was an artifact of the deep contamination — it resolves once crustal-restricted.)

**Pre/post-Tohoku split (supplementary, not the headline).** Hypothesis stated in advance: the 2011-03-11 M9 Tohoku earthquake dynamically/statically triggered a nationwide crustal-seismicity surge that is externally driven rather than self-nucleated, which should dilute mainshock-specific migration. Consistent direction (depth<=20 km, MC0.5): PRE-2011 mainshock-ref -0.091 (p0.0022), partial -0.090 (p0.002), 3D -0.100 (p0.001), gate +0.137 — strong; but its PAIRED specificity test is UNDERPOWERED (n=112, PAIRED -0.044 p0.14) so "stronger in the loaded period" is suggestive, not established. POST-2011 is weaker and the paired specificity does not reach significance, but with the present n we CANNOT distinguish genuine dilution from reduced power, so we do NOT claim the specificity "vanishes" post-Tohoku. The robust statement is the crustal-all result above; the split is a consistent-but-inconclusive supplement.

**Net (3rd region).** Foreshock migration toward the eventual mainshock is reproduced in a THIRD independent dense relocated catalogue, in Japanese inland crustal seismicity — a tectonic regime distinct from the Californian transform system — once the analysis is restricted to crustal depths (mainshock-ref -0.077 at <=20 km / -0.086 at <=15 km, mainshock-specific by the paired control, magnitude- and 3D-robust, synthetic null clean, aftershock gate passed). The effect is weak (rho ~ -0.08, ~1% of rank variance, comparable to location precision) and operationally modest, as in California, but it is now demonstrated across two independent fault systems on two continents and two plate-boundary classes of crustal faulting (California transform + Japan inland reverse/strike-slip). Cross-tectonic-regime generalisation to subduction interfaces themselves remains untested for lack of a sufficiently complete, low-Mc offshore foreshock catalogue (the Chile attempt is resolution-limited). Assets: RPi5 ~/geo-ml/juice/{migration_juice.py (baseline+MC arg), migration_juice2.py (paired test + pre/post-2011 split), migration_juice3.py (crustal depth restriction)}; catalogue JUICE.2001-2012.v1.1. Methodology identical to the Opus-reviewed SoCal test; JUICE is a replication. ## Cross-TECTONIC-REGIME test #4 — subduction megathrust (Atacama, Chile), 2026-06-25
The three positive replications are all CRUSTAL (California transform x2 + Japan inland). The one regime where the migration was originally documented qualitatively — the SUBDUCTION MEGATHRUST (Kato & Nakagawa 2012 Tohoku, Kato et al. 2016 Iquique) — had been untested at population scale for want of a dense-enough offshore foreshock catalogue (the earlier Sippl IPOC attempt FAILED the aftershock-expansion gate = resolution-limited). Re-attempted on the densest open subduction catalogue available: **Atacama segment, Chile** (Münchmeyer et al. 2025, *JGR Solid Earth*, doi:10.1029/2025JB031256; Zenodo 15083298) — 166,403 events Nov 2020–Feb 2024, deep-learning picks + GrowClust double-difference relocation, 154,485 relocated (93%), Mc ~1.5–2.0 (far below Sippl's 2.6). Its `class` column labels interface (megathrust) / intraslab / upper_plate / outer_rise / anthropogenic. Same migration spec and control suite. **R was swept a priori** (not post-hoc tuned): crustal R=20 km is too small for subduction, whose documented foreshock migration operates over ~100 km (Tohoku 180→28 km), so R∈{20,30,50,100} km was planned from the literature scale.

**The gate splits on event class — and this dense catalogue PASSES it for the megathrust interface (improving on Sippl).**
| selection | gate (aftershock-exp >0) | verdict |
|---|---|---|
| ALL classes, R50 km, MC1.5, n=235 | +0.026 (p0.12) | gate FAILS — intraslab-dominated (94k/140k); population not gate-resolvable |
| ALL classes, R100 km, MC1.5, n=211 | +0.006 (p0.58) | gate FAILS |
| **interface only, R50 km, MC1.0, M≥3.5, n=114** | **+0.079 (p1e-4)** | **gate PASSES** |
| interface only, R50 km, MC1.0, M≥4.0, n=53 | +0.109 (p0.0011) | gate passes (but underpowered for the foreshock test — see below) |

So restricting to the plate interface, the deep-learning catalogue clearly resolves aftershock expansion (p≤1e-4) — overturning the Sippl "resolution-limited" verdict: the megathrust IS now gate-resolvable with modern dense data. (Mechanism of the all-class gate failure — that intraslab aftershocks may not radially Omori-expand — is a HYPOTHESIS; we show the observation, not a class-resolved aftershock mechanism.)

**Headline (interface, R50 km, MC1.0, M≥3.5, n=114 — the only adequately-powered, gate-passing, synthetic-clean configuration):**

|---|---|---|---|---|
| aftershock GATE | +0.079 | [+0.042,+0.114] | 1e-4 | resolution OK |
| mainshock-ref | -0.033 | [-0.070,+0.002] | 0.19 | NS |
| centroid-ref | -0.031 | [-0.066,+0.005] | 0.16 | NS, ≈ mainshock-ref |

Unlike all three crustal regions (where PAIRED main-minus-centroid was significant = the contraction is specific to the eventual hypocentre), the megathrust PAIRED test is **exactly null** (-0.002, p0.99): mainshock-ref and centroid-ref are identical, so whatever weak contraction exists is GENERAL (toward the cluster centroid), NOT directed at the future mainshock. The synthetic uniform null and random-ref are clean, so this is not a pipeline/resolution artifact.

**Power (the necessary caveat).** At n=114 with the observed paired SE (~0.016), the test rejects a JUICE-strength mainshock-specificity (−0.048) at power ~0.82 (and that value lies outside the paired CI95), but is underpowered against a SoCal-strength effect (−0.032) at power ~0.48. So the honest statement is: **a CRUSTAL-STRONG (~−0.05) mainshock-specific migration is rejected on the Atacama megathrust; a CRUSTAL-WEAK (~−0.03) one cannot be ruled out with a 3.3-year catalogue.** The n=53 (M≥4) configuration is dropped from the inference as underpowered against everything (power 0.24–0.49); it serves only as a second gate-pass demonstration. For honesty: at the too-small R=30 km (where the gate does NOT pass, n=29) the raw mainshock-ref looks significant (−0.129, p0.036) but centroid-ref is equally negative (−0.126) and PAIRED is null (−0.003, p0.80) — again general contraction, not specificity, and not gate-validated.

**Net (subduction).** On the densest open subduction-megathrust catalogue, with the aftershock-expansion gate now PASSED for the interface (a genuine advance over Sippl's resolution-limited null), foreshocks show only weak GENERAL contraction toward the cluster, with NO mainshock-specific migration at crustal strength — the paired-specificity signature that defines the crustal positive is absent (JUICE-strength rejected at power 0.82; SoCal-strength underpowered). The crustal mainshock-specific migration thus does NOT extend to the ordinary subduction-megathrust population. Scope: Atacama 2020–2024 hosted no great megathrust, so these are ORDINARY M≥3.5 interface mainshocks (apples-to-apples with the crustal M≥4 populations); this does NOT refute the qualitative Kato (2012/2016) migrations on individual great-earthquake nucleations — generalisation to great-megathrust nucleation specifically is untestable with this catalogue. Assets: RPi5 ~/geo-ml/atacama/{migration_atacama.py, cat_inspect.py}; catalogue catalog_temp.csv (Zenodo 15083298). Opus-reviewed 2026-06-25: reviewer independently re-ran the configs, confirmed the numbers, ran a Monte-Carlo power analysis, and bounded the claim to "JUICE-strength specificity rejected, SoCal-strength underpowered; general contraction present; gate-mechanism a hypothesis; Kato individual sequences not negated".

## Cross-region #5 / 3rd FAULTING STYLE — central Apennines, Italy (NORMAL faulting), 2026-06-25
The three crustal positives so far span two faulting styles: California transform (SoCal, NorCal) and Japan inland reverse/strike-slip (JUICE). The third crustal style — NORMAL faulting (extension) — is added here with the central Apennines, Italy, the type-locality of normal-faulting seismicity (L'Aquila 2009 Mw6.3, Amatrice 2016 Mw6.0, Norcia Mw6.5, Visso Mw5.9). Catalogue: INGV routine bulletin via FDSN (box 42.0–43.6°N, 12.3–14.2°E, 2009–2023, M≥1.0), 181,267 events (176,814 at depth ≤20 km). **These are ABSOLUTE locations (not double-difference relocated)** — so the aftershock-expansion GATE is the decisive usability test (the absolute Sippl IPOC and even DD-Sippl FAILED it).

**Notable: an ABSOLUTE-location catalogue PASSES the gate** — a first in this series. The dense central-Italy network (incl. the 2016–17 temporary deployment) resolves within-sequence relative positions well enough: GATE aftershock-expansion +0.126 (CI95 [+0.070,+0.180], p<1e-4, n=67) at the headline setting. The aftershock-expansion gate passes in all (incl., notably, the dense absolute-location Italian catalogue). Faulting style does NOT modulate it materially — extension, compression and transform all show the same weak directed approach.

**What is bounded.** On the SUBDUCTION megathrust (Atacama, the densest open ML catalogue, gate now PASSED for the interface — overturning Sippl's resolution-limited null), the migration is **NOT mainshock-specific**: the PAIRED test is null (-0.002, p0.99 at n=114; -0.005, CI95 [-0.032,+0.031] at the power-boosted n=184), rejecting a crustal-STRONG specificity (-0.048) at power 0.93 and a crustal-WEAK (-0.032) at 0.63 -- and the n=184 CI excludes EVERY measured crustal paired value (JUICE -0.049, Apennines -0.065), so the megathrust specificity is significantly weaker than all crustal regions, not merely underpowered. The crustal-vs-subduction split (specific in crustal, general in megathrust) is itself a clue but not a mechanism proof.

**Operational status.** Weak (rho ~ -0.1 ≈ 1% of rank variance; ~ -0.2 km median approach ≈ catalogue precision) and carries NO hypocentre-forecasting skill (recency/trajectory predictors do not beat the all-foreshock centroid). Scientifically real, operationally negligible. Strictly research; no productization or alerting.

**Coverage limits / open next steps.** All results are ordinary M≥3.5-4 population statistics; generalisation to GREAT-earthquake nucleation specifically is untested (and on the one regime where it was qualitatively documented — subduction — the population signal is absent). The mechanism (aseismic vs cascade) remains the deepest open question and would need geodetic/creep data co-located with foreshock sequences, which for these small (M3-5) sequences is largely below geodetic resolution.

### Mechanism probe — weak AUXILIARY evidence only (2026-06-25)
A catalogue-only attempt to separate aseismic-slow-slip loading from cascade/triggering as the migration driver, via the burstiness B=(σ−μ)/(σ+μ) of foreshock inter-event times (steady external/aseismic loading → low B / Poissonian; Omori cascade clustering → high B). Pooling SoCal QTM + NorCal DD (n=198 sequences): migrating sequences (rho<0) are somewhat MORE bursty than non-migrating (median B 0.122 vs 0.080; Spearman(rho,B)=−0.235, p9e-4), surviving control for foreshock count (partial −0.288) and an inter-event-time-trend/acceleration proxy (partial|N,A=−0.234, p1e-3).
**This is weak, auxiliary evidence only — we do NOT assert cascade:** (i) burstiness conflates genuine clustering with the inverse-Omori acceleration of foreshocks, which cannot be cleanly separated on these short series; (ii) the pooled significance is NorCal-driven (Spearman(rho,B): NorCal −0.256 p0.002; **SoCal −0.214 p0.13, NOT significant alone** — sign-consistent only); (iii) B is unstable on 12–49-event sequences and uncalibrated. The only defensible reading is BUNDLED with the velocity result: a net approach of ~0.1 km/day (1–2 orders below SSE front speeds) AND migrating sequences being mildly more temporally clustered are both INCONSISTENT WITH A SMOOTH, STEADY aseismic-slip front driving the migration — but they cannot distinguish cascade/triggering from an accelerating (punctuated) aseismic transient. Mechanism stays unresolved; only the steady-front picture is disfavoured. Asset: ~/geo-ml/qtm/migration_mechanism.py. ### Mechanism probe 2 — repeating-earthquake CREEP proxy: spurious positive → NULL after de-confounding (2026-06-25)
A second, more direct mechanism test: do migrating sequences co-locate with aseismic CREEP? Using the Waldhauser & Schaff (2021) NorCal repeating-earthquake catalogue (27,674 repeaters — the textbook creep marker) matched to the same-network NorCal migration sequences, the local repeater density around each mainshock is a creep-activity proxy. Controlling only for local seismicity density, migrating sequences APPEARED enriched in nearby repeaters (partial Spearman(rho, repeater-count | seismicity-density) = −0.202, p0.015) — an apparent aseismic-creep signature. **But this is a GEOTHERMAL/VOLCANIC-repeater ARTIFACT:** excluding the Geysers geothermal field and Long Valley volcanic zone (induced/volcanic repeaters, not tectonic creep) collapses it to null (partial −0.041, p0.70 — the POINT ESTIMATE drops to ~zero, i.e. effect disappearance, not power loss). So in purely tectonic NorCal there is NO migration–repeater association: the creep proxy gives **no support for the aseismic-creep hypothesis**.
Caveats (necessary): this does NOT refute aseismic loading — a null is not proof of absence, and the de-confounded sample is small (n=91, only 22 non-migrating sequences), so weak effects are not excluded; and it is **NorCal-only** (the repeater catalogue exists only there; SoCal untested). Methodological lesson (kept as a neutral gotcha): controlling for seismicity density alone does NOT remove geothermal-repeater confounding — a spatial block-exclusion of induced/volcanic zones is required, otherwise a spurious creep "signal" (p0.015) appears.

### Mechanism probe 3 — migration GEOMETRY rejects a propagating aseismic slip front (2026-06-25)
The decisive geometry discriminator, calibrated against synthetic end-members. A propagating aseismic slip FRONT would make foreshocks march DIRECTIONALLY along a fault axis with the mainshock at the LEADING EDGE; cascade/static-stress concentration onto a patch makes foreshocks contract RADIALLY toward the eventual hypocentre (mainshock central). For every migrating sequence (rho<0) across the 4 crustal catalogues (n=299) we measure D=|Spearman(time, PC1 projection)| (directional progression), AR=sqrt(λ1/λ2) (cloud elongation), EDGE=2|mainshock PC1-percentile−0.5| (1=edge/front, 0=centre).
| | D (direction) | AR (elong) | EDGE (ms@edge) |
|---|---|---|---|
| REAL migrating (n=299) | **0.136** | 2.06 | **0.442** |
| synth FRONT | 0.891 | 2.19 | 0.883 |
| synth RADIAL | 0.086 | 1.15 | 0.062 |
| REAL non-migrating | 0.095 | 2.11 | 0.414 |

**A propagating slip front is REJECTED, robustly.** Real directionality (D=0.136) is far below the front end-member (0.891) and near radial (0.086); the mainshock is NOT at a leading edge (EDGE 0.442 ≪ front 0.883). A scatter sweep shows the rejection is not an idealisation artifact: to noise a synthetic front down to the real D you need lateral scatter ≈2×L (D=0.134 at 2L), but at that scatter the front has DEGENERATED into the radial end-member (AR 2.19→1.14, EDGE 0.88→0.14) — so NO front reproduces the real joint pattern (low D + elongated AR=2.06 + intermediate EDGE=0.44). The elongation (AR~2) is NOT diagnostic of a front: non-migrating sequences have the same AR (2.11), i.e. it merely reflects earthquakes lying on planar faults.
**What this resolves, and its honest limit.** This rejects a propagating aseismic slip front (the Kato-style migrating-front picture) for the crustal population — the most robust mechanism statement the data support, and it CONVERGES with the other indicators (net velocity ~0.1 km/day far below SSE fronts; weak burstiness; repeater creep-proxy null). BUT geometry distinguishes "front vs not", NOT "cascade vs non-propagating aseismic": radial contraction is equally produced by cascade/static-stress concentration onto the nucleation patch OR by a stationary (non-propagating) aseismic patch loading its surroundings. So we do NOT claim "cascade". The intermediate EDGE (0.442, well above pure-radial 0.062) leaves a WEAK residual directionality/asymmetry, not a clean point-symmetric contraction. End-member caveat: FRONT/RADIAL are cartoon isotropic-noise models (real faults branch/step), so this is a relative which-pole comparison, not absolute mechanism identification.
**Mechanism — final net.** Across four catalogue-only probes (velocity, burstiness, repeater creep-proxy, geometry) the one robust, convergent conclusion is NEGATIVE: crustal foreshock migration is **NOT driven by a propagating aseismic slip front**. The remaining candidates — cascade/static-stress concentration onto the nucleation patch vs a non-propagating aseismic transient — are not separable with open catalogue data; resolving them needs co-located geodetic/creep observation at a scale below current detection for these M≥4 sequences. Asset: ~/geo-ml/qtm/migration_geometry.py (D/AR/EDGE metrics, synthetic FRONT/RADIAL calibration, scatter sweep). Opus-reviewed (reviewer independently reproduced the metrics and the scatter crossover; conclusion bounded to "front rejected", "cascade" disclaimed, EDGE-intermediate and AR-non-diagnostic flagged, end-member idealisation noted).

### Mechanism probe 4 — Zaliapin-Ben-Zion nearest-neighbour: rejects a STEADY background/aseismic patch, but cannot reach cascade-vs-accelerating-aseismic (2026-06-25)
To separate the two survivors of the geometry test (cascade/static-triggering vs a non-propagating aseismic patch), the Zaliapin & Ben-Zion (2013) nearest-neighbour rescaled distance eta = t_yr * r_km^1.6 * 10^(-m_parent) classifies each foreshock as CLUSTERED/triggered (small eta) or BACKGROUND/Poisson (large eta). Migrating foreshocks have smaller eta = more clustered.
- **The spatial-migration CIRCULARITY is ruled out (valid control):** decomposing eta into temporal T and spatial R (q=0.5), the migrating-vs-non gap is in T (log10 gap 0.41) NOT R (gap 0.07) — so the clustering is NOT an artifact of migration=spatial-contraction (which would inflate R). Genuine temporal clustering.
- **BUT it is acceleration-confounded (BLOCKER):** migrating sequences genuinely accelerate more (fraction of foreshocks in the final 3 days 0.293 vs 0.185, p3e-4; Spearman(rho, frac_last3d)=-0.313, p7e-6). The T-gap is largely this inverse-Omori acceleration — and acceleration is produced by BOTH cascade AND an accelerating aseismic transient, so T cannot separate them.
- **Pseudo-replication corrected (BLOCKER):** the event-level p<1e-50 counts non-independent within-sequence foreshocks; at the proper SEQUENCE level (one median-eta per sequence, n=136 migrating vs 62 non) the difference is only MARGINAL/NS — MWU p=0.088 (df1.6) / 0.075 (df1.4), sign stable across df. So even "more clustered" is weak once independence is respected.
- **eta does not separate cascade from an aseismic-driven swarm:** both produce small eta (short space-time separation), so "clustered, not background" does not equal "cascade".
What it DOES establish: migrating foreshocks are not a steady Poisson/background process, which leans AGAINST a steady, non-propagating, background-rate aseismic patch — consistent with the geometry front-rejection. What it does NOT establish: cascade. Asset: ~/geo-ml/qtm/{migration_zbz.py, migration_zbz_seq.py}. Opus-reviewed (reviewer ran the acceleration test confirming the confound, flagged the pseudo-replication, and bounded the claim to "steady aseismic patch rejected, cascade-vs-accelerating-aseismic unresolved").

### Mechanism — FINAL net status (2026-06-25, all probes)
Five catalogue-only probes (velocity, burstiness, repeater creep-proxy, migration geometry, Zaliapin-Ben-Zion NN) converge on ONE robust, positive conclusion and leave one distinction open:
- **ROBUST — REJECTED: a propagating aseismic slip front.** Geometry shows no directional progression and the mainshock is not at a leading edge (no front scatter reproduces the real low-D + elongated + intermediate-EDGE pattern); net approach ~0.1 km/day is 1-2 orders below SSE front speeds. The Kato-style migrating-front picture does not describe the crustal population.
- **LEANS REJECTED: a steady, non-propagating, background-rate aseismic patch.** Foreshocks in migrating sequences are time-clustered/accelerating, not a steady Poisson background (Z-BZ, though seq-level only marginal).
- **UNRESOLVED with open catalogue data: cascade/static-stress concentration vs an ACCELERATING aseismic transient.** Both produce radial geometry, temporal clustering and terminal acceleration; no catalogue-only statistic (geometry/Z-BZ/burstiness) separates them, and the repeater creep-proxy is null (no creep co-location, power-limited, geothermal-confounded). Separating them requires detecting the aseismic slip itself — co-located geodetic/creepmeter observation — which for these M>=3.5-4 sequences is below current open-data resolution; it is feasible only for the largest (M6+) crustal sequences with dense co-located GNSS, the concrete next target. The migration is therefore established as a radial contraction toward the eventual hypocentre that is NOT a propagating slip front; whether the radial concentration is cascade or an accelerating aseismic transient is the remaining open question.

### Mechanism — CAPSTONE (2026-06-25, Opus-adjudicated, supersedes the per-probe nets above)

- **ROBUST, the one firm conclusion — a propagating aseismic slip FRONT is rejected.** Migration geometry shows no directional progression and the mainshock is not at a leading edge (no front scatter reproduces the real low-D/elongated/intermediate-EDGE pattern); the net approach ~0.1 km/day is 1-2 orders below SSE front speeds. ### Mechanism — REVISED capstone (2026-06-25, literature correction; supersedes the "disfavour aseismic" framing above)
A literature check overturned an over-conclusion in the probe-5 / capstone framing above. **CORRECTION:** the project's GNSS nulls (Ridgecrest here; Kumamoto in RESULT_gnss_kumamoto) are DETECTABILITY FLOORS, not evidence against aseismic slip — the Kumamoto synthetic-injection floor is Mw~5.6-5.9 (S*=0.08-0.25 m on a 14x10 km patch). Using the more-sensitive TILTMETER channel (tilt = displacement gradient, resolves smaller slip than GNSS at the same spacing), **Yue et al. (2026, Earth Planet. Sci. Lett. 681, doi:10.1016/j.epsl.2026.119905)** report, for the 2016 Kumamoto large case (GPS+tiltmeter), an afterslip (Mw~6.1) AND a transient slow-slip event (Mw~5.5, per abstract) beginning ~9 h after the M6 foreshock near the eventual mainshock hypocentre, quasi-statically loading and facilitating nucleation — a "fourth foreshock mode"; a Nature 2026 companion (Foreshock-induced slip transients set mainshock nucleation timing, s41586-026-10497-5) reports the same class of phenomenon. The reported Mw~5.5 transient sits BELOW the project's Mw~5.6-5.9 GNSS floor, so the GNSS null is genuinely uninformative about it. **The earlier "large aseismic transient disfavoured (n=2)" was an over-reading of GNSS nulls below their own detection floor — WITHDRAWN.** (Same lesson as the repeater spurious positive, applied to a false NEGATIVE: a sub-floor null is not absence.)
**Revised net (bounded, attributions explicit):**
- **ROBUST:** a propagating aseismic slip FRONT is rejected (geometry; low net migration speed only re-confirms "not a front", not a positive for any driver).
- **LARGE CASE (literature-observed, n=1 well-resolved):** an aseismic transient slow-slip near the hypocentre facilitates nucleation by quasi-static loading (Yue et al. 2026, tiltmeter; Kumamoto). Consistent with — not my proof of — a NON-PROPAGATING aseismic slow-slip patch as driver.
- **POPULATION (my 4-region migration, M4-5):** the radial geometry and the temporal-not-spatial Z-BZ clustering (R/T asymmetry) are CONSISTENT with such aseismic-patch loading, BUT the equivalent population transients (Mw~3-4) are below ALL instruments (GNSS and tiltmeter) — so this is an INFERENCE from large-case observation + population geometry, NOT a population observation; my catalogue data alone cannot separate aseismic loading from cascade.
- **INTEGRATION (cascade re-positioned, not withdrawn):** aseismic and triggered seismicity are NOT exclusive — a quasi-static aseismic loading transient naturally drives a subordinate, time-clustered triggered-seismicity response (Yue et al. note "nearby seismic activity acceleration" accompanying the transient). So the Z-BZ time-clustering is plausibly the triggered response to aseismic loading rather than an independent cascade. We do NOT elevate "aseismic patch" to "most likely" (symmetric to having declined "cascade most likely"); the bounded statement is: front rejected (robust); large-case aseismic transient observed (literature); population consistent-but-not-observed; aseismic-loading-with-subordinate-triggering is the coherent integrated picture, with cascade-vs-aseismic primacy not separable in my data.

### Literature placement — aseismic-nucleation is multi-case, but mostly PROPAGATING-FRONT type; my population is the non-front exception (2026-06-25)
Aseismic slip transients in the foreshock-to-mainshock transition are a published MULTI-CASE phenomenon — but the honest placement of my population result is one of CONTRAST, not confirmation, because the documented cases are mostly the propagating-front type my geometry rejected:
- **Tohoku 2011 (Kato et al. 2012, Science):** two foreshock sequences migrate along the trench at **2-10 km/day** toward the epicentre; repeating-earthquake-inferred quasi-static slow slip propagates to the rupture point — a fast PROPAGATING FRONT.
- **Iquique 2014 (Ruiz et al. 2014, Science; Kato & Nakagawa 2014, GRL):** along-strike foreshock migration (~tens of km, days) with multiple slow-slip events and a GPS-detected SSE in the mainshock area — again a PROPAGATING FRONT (the "~270-day up-dip acceleration" figure is from secondary sources and not re-verified here).
- **Kumamoto 2016 (Yue et al. 2026, EPSL 681):** GPS+tiltmeter resolve a transient slow slip (Mw~5.5 per abstract) near the hypocentre facilitating nucleation — a crustal case; and **Kato et al. (2016, GRL) report the Kumamoto foreshocks migrating along strike/dip (directional, propagating afterslip).**
- **Nature 2026 (s41586-026-10497-5):** a laboratory + rate-and-state THEORY framework (foreshock-induced transient sliding velocity sets nucleation duration/length) — a mechanism plausibility argument, not a natural-population observation.

**Two honest tensions, stated not papered over:**
1. The published large-case migrations are predominantly PROPAGATING FRONTS (2-10 km/day) — exactly the geometry my analysis REJECTS for the crustal population (radial, ~0.1 km/day, mainshock not at a leading edge). So my population is NOT the "signature" of those front phenomena; calling it that would contradict my own front-rejection.
2. **CORRECTION (factual):** the documented large cases are NOT in my catalogues at all — Kumamoto is 2016 and my Japan catalogue (JUICE) ends 2012; Tohoku/Iquique are megathrust; my four catalogues are SoCal/NorCal (California), JUICE Japan (<=2012, crustal), Apennines (Italy). So there is no SAME-SEQUENCE contradiction. The tension is between the LITERATURE's large cases (Tohoku/Iquique megathrust and the crustal Kumamoto, all M>=6.5, all described as directional/propagating fronts incl. Kato 2016 GRL for Kumamoto) and my SEPARATE M4-5 crustal population (radial, non-front). The most parsimonious reading is SCALE-DEPENDENT migration character: large nucleation (M>=6.5) propagates as a front (2-10 km/day), while small M4-5 sequences show radial, near-stationary concentration (~0.1 km/day) — different sequences, different scale, not a contradiction in one sequence. Whether a per-sequence radial metric would also under-resolve a weak along-strike component in the large cases is untested (they are outside my catalogues).

**What is shared, and what is mine.** The only proposition shared across all of these and my work is the broad MECHANISM CATEGORY — that an aseismic slip transient can participate in the foreshock-to-mainshock transition. The observable geometry (front vs radial), speed (2-10 km/day vs ~0.1 km/day) and scale (megathrust/large-crustal vs M4-5 population) all DIFFER. My contribution is therefore NOT a "population extension of the multi-case front phenomenon" but rather (a) the population-statistic-isation of the qualitative single-case migration observation across 466 sequences / 4 dense catalogues / 3 faulting styles, and (b) the geometric demonstration that, at the population scale, the migration is radial and NOT a propagating front. My population is at most a pattern CONSISTENT with the aseismic-loading mechanism category (an inference, since my data cannot directly observe the sub-instrumental Mw~3-4 population transients or separate aseismic loading from cascade), and is in explicit geometric tension with the front-type descriptions that dominate the documented large cases.
