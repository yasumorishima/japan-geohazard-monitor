# High-resolution verification — Shelly (2020) catalogue re-test of the Ridgecrest nucleation nulls

## Motivation
The open 17-station SCSN PhaseNet pipeline returned all-null for foreshock acceleration (Ridgecrest test 18) and the b-value foreshock-traffic-light (test 19) on the 2019 Ridgecrest M6.4 to M7.1 sequence, with a standing caveat that genuine progress needs denser data. This re-tests every null against the densest OPEN catalogue available — Shelly (2020, SRL; USGS data release doi:10.5066/P9JN6H0N): 34,091 template-matched, relocated, calibrated events over 2019-07-04 to 07-16, covering the M6.4 to M7.1 foreshock window — to determine whether the nulls were resolution-limited or real.

## Catalogue and method
34,091 events, magnitude -0.34 to 7.10, Mc (maximum-curvature +0.2) = 1.10 (vs the open pipeline ~1.8). Precise relocations and calibrated magnitudes. Pure catalogue statistics (no waveforms), on RPi5: Aki-Utsu and Van der Elst (2021) b-positive b-values; A5 powered last-Xh + A6 Omori-conditioned acceleration tests; FTLS B1/B2/B3.

## Results — every temporal null confirmed (and strengthened)
- **B1 (FTLS foreshock-vs-aftershock b)**: M6.4-phase (the M7.1 foreshocks) b_Aki = 0.72 +/- 0.01 / b+ = 0.85 (n = 4369) vs M7.1-aftershocks b_Aki = 0.68 / b+ = 0.87 (n = 29077). The foreshock-phase b is NOT lower than the aftershock b — against the Gulia and Wiemer foreshock-b-drop premise, now at 30k-event resolution. (Pre-M6.4 background n = 36, still too few — the one resolution limit that persists.)
- **B2 (prospective temporal b+)**: flat (Theil-Sen +0.0005/h, Spearman rho = 0.07, p = 0.64); final-6h / 3h b+ = 0.79 / 0.79. No prospective b-drop toward M7.1.
- **A5 (rate end-loading)**: 574 events >= Mc in the final 12 h (47.8/h); last-3h obs = 107 vs uniform exp = 143.5 (binom and perm p = 1.000) — the rate DECREASES toward M7.1, no end-loading.
- **A6 (Omori-conditioned)**: at high resolution the M6.4-aftershock Omori decay is now resolved (fit p = 0.65); predict-late last-3h exp = 143.3, obs = 107, Poisson p = 0.999 — no end-window excess even against the decaying baseline.

## B3 (spatial b-asperity) — null-consistent, not a detection
With precise locations the M7.1 nucleation patch falls at the low-b extreme of the nearest-neighbour b+ field (percentile 0.04 at K=100, 0.06 at K=200; a random-center null confirms 0.042). BUT two controls discount it: (i) a location-permutation control shows the field dispersion (std 0.166) is indistinguishable from random grouping (null 0.166, p = 0.98) — the spatial b+ field is UNRESOLVED at these patch sizes, its dispersion being b-positive sampling noise; (ii) the M7.1-patch b+ = 0.60 has a bootstrap 95% CI of 0.45 to 0.85 (only 51 positive magnitude differences at K=100) that INCLUDES the field median (0.83) — not significantly low. So the low percentile is an a-posteriori sampling-noise draw, not a resolved low-b asperity. Compared with Kumamoto B3 (nucleation patch ABOVE median b), Ridgecrest is at the low end but within noise — neither case yields a resolved prospective low-b asperity. A classic b-map on the full calibrated catalogue is the documented next step for any definitive spatial claim.

## Conclusion — the "resolution-limited" escape hatch is closed for the temporal channels
The densest open catalogue available (34,091 events, Mc 1.10, precise relocations, calibrated magnitudes) reproduces every temporal null from the open 17-station pipeline: foreshock-phase b not depressed relative to aftershocks, no prospective b-drop, and no rate acceleration (the rate declines into the M7.1, whether tested against a uniform or a fitted-Omori baseline). Higher resolution did NOT convert any temporal null to a detection, so the Ridgecrest nucleation nulls are robust to resolution rather than artifacts of a coarse pipeline. The spatial b-channel (B3) remains null-consistent (the M7.1-patch low-b ranking is within sampling noise and the field is unresolved). The honest, generalisable result: even the best open high-resolution catalogue does not prospectively mark the Ridgecrest M7.1 nucleation in foreshock rate or b-value.
