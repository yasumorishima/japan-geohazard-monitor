# Synthesis of the FTLS b-value channel: five cases, the incompleteness mechanism, and the limits of b-positive

## What this consolidates
The arc applied the Gulia and Wiemer (2019, Nature) Foreshock-Traffic-Light System (FTLS: a foreshock-phase b-value DROP relative to background = a RED light predicting a larger impending event) to five mainshocks with independent / incompleteness-robust estimators: Kumamoto 2016 (M6.5->M7.3), Ridgecrest 2019 (M6.4->M7.1), Amatrice-Visso-Norcia 2016 (Mw6.5), L'Aquila 2009 (Mw6.3), and Iquique 2014 (Mw8.1, subduction megathrust). All five returned NULL on a prospective temporal b-precursor under the Van der Elst (2021) b-positive estimator. This note states the one robust cross-case finding, a controlled experiment on the mechanism, and -- importantly -- the limits of both the mechanism claim and of b-positive itself. It introduces no new method; it diagnoses a published prediction system and delineates a known estimator's robustness boundary.

## The robust cross-case finding
The FTLS RED is operationally an Aki maximum-likelihood b-value DROP. Across all five cases the Aki foreshock value is low (the apparent RED), but the incompleteness-robust b-positive shows the foreshock b is NOT anomalously low relative to the comparison sequence: the foreshock-vs-aftershock b-positive difference is small in every case (of order -5%, with bootstrap CIs that overlap), an order of magnitude below the Aki "drop" against a quiescent background (-27% to -34% where a background is even measurable). This comparative result is the durable one, and it is robust to estimator nuisance parameters (next section). No case shows an FTLS-magnitude prospective foreshock b-drop under b-positive; prospective trends are flat or rising; rate "accelerations" reduce to ordinary Omori/ETAS cascade triggering; and no case has a spatially-resolved low-b nucleation asperity.

## Controlled mechanism experiment (sufficiency, not field proof)
On the dense Tan et al. (2021) Amatrice catalogue, one complete population (aftershocks M>=1.0, n=60,548; true Aki@1.0 = 0.88) was randomly split into halves A and B (identical true b by construction). B was left complete; A was given a SHARP elevated completeness (keep M >= Mc_imp). Computing Aki at the FIXED nominal analysis Mc=1.0 -- exactly the error an FTLS analyst makes when the active sequence is less complete than the catalogue nominal Mc -- collapses Aki_A to 0.45 / 0.30 / 0.23 / 0.18 at Mc_imp 1.5 / 2.0 / 2.5 / 3.0 (vs B = 0.88), reproducing a RED-sized drop; Aki at the CORRECT Mc recovers (~0.93-1.10); and b-positive_A stays 1.15-1.33 (no spurious drop; the mild upward drift with Mc_imp is magnitude-range dependence plus reduced-n variance, an UPWARD = conservative direction). This proves the analysis-Mc artifact is SUFFICIENT to manufacture the FTLS Aki RED while leaving b-positive flat. It is a sufficiency demonstration, not proof that this specific mechanism produced each field RED (see Limits).

## The limit of b-positive (the genuinely new methodological point)
b-positive is robust to a SHARP completeness threshold, even a time-varying one, because consecutive positive magnitude differences are preserved when every event above a (possibly moving) Mc is detected -- confirmed above and in the time-varying STAI-burst variant (Aki biased, b+ ~stable). It is NOT robust to a SMOOTH magnitude-dependent detection roll-off: imposing a logistic keep-probability p(M)=1/(1+exp(-(M-Mc)/0.3)) on half A drives BOTH Aki AND b-positive down (b+ 1.21 -> 0.90 / 0.76 / 0.64 / 0.56 at Mc 1.5 / 2.0 / 2.5 / 3.0). Real catalogue incompleteness near Mc is gradual, not perfectly sharp, so b-positive is only APPROXIMATELY robust in practice; under a smooth roll-off it is biased LOW. The direction matters: a smooth roll-off that is WORSE in the foreshock window (plausible -- detection degrades as the network is swamped approaching the mainshock) would bias the foreshock b-positive LOW, i.e. TOWARD a spurious RED. The five nulls were obtained despite this conservative pressure, so they are robust to it; the residual threat is the opposite asymmetry (a worse-completeness AFTERSHOCK window masking a real foreshock drop), which the small, CI-overlapping foreshock-vs-aftershock differences do not support.

## Estimator nuisance: dM threshold and continuous magnitudes
For continuous-magnitude catalogues (Amatrice/Tan, ml to 4 decimals) the b-positive ABSOLUTE value is dM-threshold-dependent: using dM>0 includes near-zero differences and inflates b+ (Amatrice foreshock b+ = 1.12 at dM>0 vs 0.89 at dM>=0.1 vs 0.73 at dM>=0.2). The canonical Van der Elst form uses dM >= a finite increment; the earlier Amatrice/Norcia absolute b+ values (reported at dM>0) are correspondingly high. CRUCIALLY the foreshock-vs-aftershock COMPARISON -- the actual FTLS test -- is common-mode stable: the drop is -6.9% / -4.9% / -3.6% at dM>0 / 0.1 / 0.2, small and same-signed throughout. The 0.1-binned catalogues (L'Aquila/Cabrera, Iquique/Sippl) are unaffected (dM>0 == dM>=0.1). So the conclusions (no robust drop) stand; only the Amatrice absolute b+ magnitudes are threshold-sensitive.

## Limits and honest scope
- **Mechanism is sufficiency, not field-proven.** A per-case field analog (max-curvature Mc by phase) found the foreshock-phase Mc is NOT systematically elevated above the aftershock Mc (Amatrice 0.0 vs 0.2; Iquique 2.4 vs 2.6; L'Aquila foreshock 0.9 vs early-swarm 0.7), and computing Aki at each phase own Mc does not remove the (small) foreshock-vs-aftershock drop. So the controlled artifact explains the LARGE foreshock-vs-quiescent-background RED, but the foreshock-vs-aftershock contrast in these cases is intrinsically small rather than an Mc-misspecification artifact. The durable claim is the b-positive comparative null, not a universal single mechanism.
- **Case series, not a population test** of FTLS skill; the five are selected notable mainshocks (and the two G and W showcases). No statement is made about FTLS hit-rate in general.
- **No new estimator.** This confirms and applies Van der Elst (2021) threshold-robustness to diagnose the FTLS RED pattern, and delineates where that robustness breaks (smooth roll-off).
- **Falsifier:** a case where the foreshock Mc demonstrably equals the aftershock Mc, the catalogue is complete, yet the Aki foreshock b drops by an FTLS-RED margin AND b-positive drops with it (beyond CI), would break the artifact explanation and indicate a real foreshock b decrease.
