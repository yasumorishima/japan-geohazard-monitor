# Independent b-value / Foreshock-Traffic-Light + acceleration test — 2014 Iquique Mw8.1 (subduction megathrust)

## Motivation
The 2014 Iquique (Pisagua) Mw8.1 (Mw8.2 in some solutions), northern Chile megathrust, is the canonical "accelerated nucleation" foreshock case: a ~2-week intense, MIGRATING foreshock sequence with documented precursory SLOW SLIP (Ruiz et al. 2014 Science; Kato and Nakagawa 2014 GRL), a seismicity-rate acceleration starting ~270 d before at Mc~3.8 up-dip (Kato et al. 2016 Sci Rep), and a multi-year b-value decline to ~0.6 with a terminal reversal (Schurr et al. 2014 Nature). This arc earlier tested the precursory slow slip geodetically and found it at/below open geodetic resolution. Here we extend the FTLS b-value/rate channel from crustal cases (Kumamoto, Ridgecrest, Norcia, L'Aquila) to a subduction megathrust, and ask specifically whether the operational SHORT-TERM FTLS b/rate SEISMICITY signatures are present. This is the fifth b-channel case and the first megathrust.

Mainshock MS = 2014-04-01 23:46:47 UTC, 19.59N(S) 70.94W (ML8.1 in catalogue is a saturated local magnitude, not the event size). Largest early foreshock Mw6.6, 2014-03-16 21:16 (16.1 d before); further Mw6.1-6.4 foreshocks on 03-22/03-23 and a 03-24 swarm.

## Method and scope (open data)
Catalogue: Sippl et al. (2018) IPOC double-difference catalogue (GFZ Data Services 10.5880/GFZ.4.1.2018.001, CC BY 4.0), 101,602 events 2007-2014, ML, northern Chile forearc. Spatially restricted to the Iquique source box (lat -20.8..-18.8, lon -71.6..-70.2): 6,215 events, 2,623 pre-mainshock. Offshore megathrust + onshore-network geometry gives in-box pre-MS Mc = 2.60 -- higher than the crustal cases, intrinsic to the setting. Estimators: Aki MLE + Van der Elst (2021) b-positive (incompleteness-robust), bootstrap 95% CI, b+ reported only at npos >= 25. Phases at common Mc (2.5 and 3.0): background [2007, 2014-01), foreshock [Mw6.6, MS), foreshock FS-EXCLUDED [Mw6.6+1d, MS) (Omori-burst guard), aftershock [MS+6h, end] -- all within the same catalogue.

SCOPE (set up front, because Iquique has among the best-documented precursors in the literature): this tests the short-term FTLS b/rate SEISMICITY channel at open completeness within the tested windows. It does NOT test, and does not refute, (i) the Kato et al. (2016) ~270-day, Mc~3.8, up-dip template-matched seismicity acceleration, (ii) the documented aseismic slow slip / repeating-earthquake / migration precursors, or (iii) the Schurr et al. (2014) multi-year b-decline -- these are longer-timescale, denser-catalogue, up-dip, or aseismic observables. Migration is a spatio-temporal/aseismic-slip signature out of scope here (covered by the arc's geodetic slow-slip null).

## Results

### B1 — no FTLS foreshock b-drop; the small foreshock-vs-aftershock gap is the Schurr long-term decline, not a RED
| phase | n(Mc2.5) | Aki(2.5) | b+(2.5)[CI] | b+(3.0)[CI] |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| background [2007, 2014-01) | 1058 | 0.61 | 0.65 [0.60,0.70] | 0.68 [0.61,0.75] |
| foreshock [Mw6.6, MS) | 681 | 0.44 | 0.60 [0.55,0.65] | 0.60 [0.53,0.67] |
| foreshock FS-EXCLUDED | 532 | 0.47 | 0.62 [0.56,0.69] | 0.62 [0.54,0.72] |
| aftershock [MS+6h, end] | 2517 | 0.49 | 0.59 [0.56,0.62] | 0.64 [0.61,0.69] |

The Aki foreshock value (0.44) is 28% below background, but that is an incompleteness artifact: the b-positive foreshock value is 0.60 and is stable under FS-exclusion (0.62). A completeness check shows the AFTERSHOCK b+ rises with Mc (0.59 / 0.64 / 0.70 / 0.77 at Mc 2.5 / 3.0 / 3.5 / 4.0), i.e. it is itself incompleteness-biased low at Mc 2.5, whereas the foreshock b+ is stable (0.60 / 0.60 / 0.64 at Mc 2.5 / 3.0 / 3.5). At the cleaner Mc 3.0-3.5 the foreshock b+ (0.60-0.64) sits marginally BELOW the aftershock (0.64-0.70) and below the long-term background (0.68) -- a ~0.05-0.08 difference, directionally consistent with the multi-year b-decline to ~0.6 documented by Schurr et al. (2014), but with overlapping CIs and an order of magnitude too small to function as an operational FTLS foreshock RED light. There is no FTLS-magnitude foreshock b-drop.

### B2 — prospective b flat-to-rising, not dropping
A non-overlapping moving b+ over the acute window (N=80, 8 windows) trends slightly UPWARD toward the mainshock (Theil-Sen +0.0139/day, Spearman rho = +0.857, p = 0.007) -- the opposite of the FTLS-required terminal drop, and directionally consistent with the terminal b-reversal Schurr et al. (2014) report in the final days (though 8 windows is too few to assert the trend independently). The final 5 d b+ = 0.52 [0.36,0.77] (npos 32); the final 3 d / 1 d are unmeasurable (npos 19 / 6) because the seismicity rate is low in the final week. No prospective b-drop.

### RATE — acute-window seismicity decays; no short-term acceleration excess over triggering
The acute-window rate (Mc 2.5, 681 events, 16.1 d, ~1 d bins toward MS) is burst-driven: a Mw6.6 burst (149 day 1), decay, a secondary burst at -8 to -10 d (the 03-22/03-24 Mw6.1-6.4 foreshocks), then a QUIET final week (day -8..-1 = 58,25,22,6,17,15,16,16). The overall trend is DECAY (Spearman rate-vs-time-toward-MS rho = -0.731, p = 0.001). An ETAS-lite Omori superposition (mu + sum over M>=5 triggers, A*10^(mi-Mc)/(t-ti+c)^p) fit to the early window and extrapolated finds the final 2-3 d rate consistent with triggered decay (final 3 d predict 42.8, obs 47, ratio 1.10, Poisson p(excess) = 0.28; final 2 d ratio 1.12, p = 0.28) -- no significant short-term acceleration excess over the foreshock-triggered cascade. SCOPE: this is the 16-day acute window at Mc 2.5; the monthly in-box rate (Mc 3.0) over the 9 months before MS is flat (3-40/month) until the final month, i.e. this catalogue does not reach the Kato et al. (2016) ~270-day, Mc~3.8, up-dip template-matched acceleration, which is a denser/longer/up-dip observable and is neither tested nor refuted here.

### B3 — no resolved low-b nucleation asperity
Foreshock spatial b+ field (681 acute foreshocks), magnitude-permutation control: K=60 MS-patch b+ 0.58 vs field median 0.57 (percentile 0.57, control p = 0.975); K=100 patch 0.51 vs median 0.58 (percentile 0.16, control p = 1.000). The field is statistically UNRESOLVED at both K (observed dispersion not above the location-shuffled null), so the K-sensitive patch percentile is ranking noise. No resolved low-b nucleation asperity.

## Conclusion
The 2014 Iquique Mw8.1 -- the canonical accelerated-nucleation megathrust foreshock sequence -- shows NO short-term FTLS b/rate SEISMICITY precursor at open completeness: the foreshock b-positive (~0.60) is not anomalously low (the Aki 0.44 drop is incompleteness, robust to FS-exclusion; the marginal foreshock-below-aftershock gap at clean Mc is the small Schurr 2014 long-term decline, not an FTLS RED); the prospective b is flat-to-rising (consistent with the documented terminal b-reversal); the acute-window seismicity rate decays and shows no significant acceleration excess over Omori cascade triggering; and there is no resolved low-b nucleation asperity. This is NOT a claim that Iquique had no precursor -- it has among the best-documented precursors in the literature. The point is sharper: those precursors live in the ASEISMIC / moment / migration / long-timescale / up-dip domain (slow slip, repeating earthquakes, the ~270-day Mc~3.8 acceleration, the multi-year b-decline) -- observables that are at or below open-data resolution and outside the operational short-term FTLS b/rate seismicity channel, which therefore does NOT capture this nucleation.

This is the fifth case on the FTLS b-value channel (Kumamoto M6.5->M7.3, Ridgecrest M6.4->M7.1, Amatrice-Visso-Norcia Mw6.5, L'Aquila Mw6.3, Iquique Mw8.1), all null on a prospective short-term temporal b/rate seismicity precursor, and the first on a subduction megathrust. Open-completeness limitation, stated precisely: the foreshock-period offshore completeness floor is Mc ~2.5-2.7 (the temporary network densified only after the Mw6.6, and the densest open template-matched catalogue, Kato et al. 2016, trades depth-of-detection for Mc ~3.8), so a sub-Mc-2.5 pre-Mw6.6 offshore foreshock catalogue is not openly available; the b+ null is incompleteness-robust by construction, but the rate null is NOT projected below Mc 2.5. Scope: a case series of selected notable mainshocks, not a population test of FTLS skill.
